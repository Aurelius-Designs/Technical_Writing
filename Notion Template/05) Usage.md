In this section, we'll explore how to use our web application effectively. Whether you're a new user or an experienced one, this section will guide you through various aspects of the application's usage.

## **User Interface**

### **Dashboard Overview**

Upon logging in, you will land on your user dashboard. Here's a brief overview of what you'll find:

- **Navigation Menu**: The menu on the left allows you to switch between different sections of the application, including your profile, content, and settings.
- **Profile Information**: Click on your profile picture or name to access and edit your personal information.
- **Notifications**: Keep an eye on your notifications, including updates and messages.
- **Recent Activity**: Review your recent actions and activities within the application.

### **Creating and Managing Content**

One of the primary uses of our web application is content creation and management. Here's how you can get started:

1. **Creating Content**:
    - Click the "New" button or similar to create a new item, whether it's an article, document, image, or another content type.
    - Follow the prompts to add content, including text, media, and attachments.
2. **Editing Content**:
    - Navigate to the content you want to edit and click the "Edit" button.
    - Make your changes and save them.
3. **Organizing Content**:
    - Use folders or categories to organize your content.
    - Add tags or labels to make content easier to find.

### **Collaboration and Sharing**

Our application makes it easy to collaborate with others:

- **Sharing Content**:
    - Click the "Share" button on a piece of content to invite others to collaborate.
    - Specify the access level (view, edit, comment) for each collaborator.
- **Real-Time Collaboration**:
    - When multiple users are editing the same document, you'll see real-time updates from collaborators.

## **User Roles and Permissions**

Our web application supports various user roles and permissions to control access and actions within the system. Here are some common roles:

- **Admin**: The administrator role has full access and can manage users, settings, and content.
- **Editor**: Editors can create and edit content but may have limited access to system settings.
- **User**: Regular users can create and manage their content, collaborate with others, and follow content updates.
